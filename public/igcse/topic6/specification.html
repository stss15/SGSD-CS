Topic 6: Automated and Emerging Technologies

Complete Specification & Learning Guide

IGCSE Computer Science 0478

The Future is Automated

From the sensors that control the temperature in your home to the AI algorithms that recommend your next movie, automated technologies are reshaping our world. In this topic, you will explore how computers sense the world, how robots are built, and how artificial intelligence simulates human thought.

What You Will Learn

In this section, we cover three main areas of emerging technology:

  • 6.1 Automated Systems: How sensors, microprocessors, and actuators work together.
  • 6.2 Robotics: The characteristics, roles, and impact of robots in society.
  • 6.3 Artificial Intelligence: Understanding AI, Expert Systems, and Machine Learning.

6.1 Automated Systems

What you need to know:

  • System Components: Describe how sensors, microprocessors and actuators can be used in collaboration to create automated systems.
    • Understand the loop: Input (Sensors) -> Process (Microprocessor compares data to pre-set values) -> Output (Actuators perform action).
  • Scenario Analysis: Describe the advantages and disadvantages of an automated system used for a given scenario.
    • Consider factors like safety (hazardous environments), speed/consistency (24/7 operation), accuracy, cost (setup vs running), and social impact (job replacement).

Notes & Guidance

  • Key Components:
    • Sensors: Temperature, Light, Moisture, Motion (Infra-red), Gas, pH, Pressure.
    • Microprocessor: The "brain" that reads sensor data, compares it to stored values, and sends signals.
    • Actuators: Motors (movement), Pumps (water), Heaters (temperature), Valves (gas/liquid), LED/Bulbs (light), Buzzers (sound).
  • The Feedback Loop: Automated systems often use a continuous loop where the output affects the input (e.g., A heater turns on -> room gets hotter -> sensor reads higher temp -> heater turns off).
  • Scenarios:
    • Industry: Nuclear power control, car manufacturing robots.
    • Transport: Self-driving cars, adaptive cruise control, train track monitoring.
    • Agriculture: Automated irrigation (watering) systems, smart greenhouses.
    • Domestic: Smart thermostats, robotic vacuum cleaners, washing machines.
    • Science: Monitoring experiments over long periods (e.g., pH levels in a river).

6.2 Robotics

What you need to know:

  • Definition: Understand what is meant by robotics.
    • Robotics is a branch of computer science that incorporates the design, construction, and operation of robots.
  • Characteristics: Describe the characteristics of a robot.
    • A robot typically has a mechanical structure, electrical components (sensors, microprocessors, actuators), and is programmable.
  • Roles & Impact: Understand the roles that robots can perform and describe the advantages and disadvantages of their use.
    • Analyze their use in specific sectors like industry, transport, and medicine.

Notes & Guidance

  • The 3 Key Characteristics:
    • 1. Mechanical Structure: The frame, arms, wheels, or chassis that gives the robot its physical form.
    • 2. Electrical Components:
      • Sensors: To perceive the environment (cameras, lidar, ultrasonic).
      • Microprocessor: The controller that processes data and makes decisions.
      • Actuators: To create movement (motors, hydraulics) or action.
    • 3. Programmable: It follows a set of instructions (code) to perform tasks, which can be changed to perform different tasks.
  • Sectors & Examples:
    • Industry: Welding, painting, assembly lines (High precision, heavy lifting).
    • Transport: Self-driving trucks, drones for delivery, warehouse robots.
    • Agriculture: Harvesting crops, spraying pesticides (reduces waste), monitoring soil.
    • Medicine: Surgical robots (precision), prosthetics, pharmacy dispensing.
    • Domestic: Vacuum cleaners, lawn mowers, service robots.
    • Entertainment: Theme park animatronics, toy robots.

6.3 Artificial Intelligence

What you need to know:

  • Definition: Understand what is meant by artificial intelligence (AI).
    • AI is a branch of computer science dealing with the simulation of intelligent behaviours by computers.
  • Characteristics: Describe the main characteristics of AI.
    • Collection of data, rules for using that data, ability to reason, and the ability to learn and adapt.
  • AI Systems: Explain the basic operation and components of AI systems to simulate intelligent behaviour.
    • Focus on Expert Systems and Machine Learning.

Notes & Guidance

  • Expert Systems: A computer system that emulates the decision-making ability of a human expert.
    • Knowledge Base: A database of facts and rules.
    • Rule Base: A set of rules (IF-THEN logic) used to make decisions.
    • Inference Engine: The program that applies the rules to the knowledge base and user inputs to deduce new information.
    • Interface: How the user interacts with the system (inputs queries, receives answers).
  • Machine Learning (ML):
    • When a program has the ability to automatically adapt its own processes and/or data.
    • Instead of being explicitly programmed with every rule, the system is "trained" on large datasets to recognize patterns and improve over time.
  • AI Characteristics:
    • Data Collection: Gathering vast amounts of information.
    • Reasoning: Using logic to draw conclusions from data.
    • Learning: Improving performance based on past experiences/data.

Exam Expectations: What You Need to Do

For the IGCSE Computer Science exam, you must be able to:

  • Analyze Scenarios: Given a real-world situation (e.g., a smart greenhouse), identify the sensors and actuators needed and explain the process.
  • Evaluate Impact: Discuss the pros and cons of replacing humans with robots in specific industries.
  • Differentiate AI: Clearly distinguish between standard programming, expert systems, and machine learning.
  • Identify Components: Label or describe the parts of an expert system (Knowledge Base, Inference Engine, etc.).
