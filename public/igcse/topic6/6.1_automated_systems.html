The Invisible Workforce

The Scenario

You walk towards a shopping mall door. It opens. You didn't touch anything.

The Reality

A computer "saw" you, "decided" to let you in, and "pushed" the door.

Key Idea: This is an Automated System.

What is an Automated System?

Automated System

A combination of software and hardware designed to work automatically without human intervention.

It relies on the interaction between Sensors, Microprocessors, and Actuators.

Deep Dive: The Control Loop

The Control Loop

1. Input (Sensors)
Sensors measure the environment (e.g., Temperature).
Analogue data is converted to Digital via ADC.
2. Process (Microprocessor)
Reads data. Compares it to Pre-set Values.
"Is Temp > 25°C?"
3. Output (Actuators)
If the condition is met, a signal is sent to an Actuator.
e.g., Turn on Fan. (DAC used if analogue output needed).
4. Feedback
The loop repeats. The sensor checks if the action worked.
"Is it cool enough yet?"

Case Study 1: Industry

How do we manage safety in a Nuclear Power Station where reaction times must be in microseconds?

Deep Dive: Nuclear Control

Nuclear Power Control

Sensors
Temperature, Pressure, Radiation, Gas levels.
Microprocessor
DCS (Distributed Control System). Compares data to safety limits.
Actuators
Pumps (water), Valves (gas), Rods (shutdown).
Why Automate?

Humans are too slow to react to a meltdown. Robots can work in radioactive areas.

Case Study 2: Transport

How does a self-parking car "see" the curb without eyes?

Deep Dive: Self-Parking & Cruise Control

Self-Parking Cars

The Sensors

Ultrasonic/Infrared: Transmit signals and measure the time for the "bounce back" to calculate distance.

Cameras: Identify lines and objects.

The Actuators

Steering: Motor turns the wheel.

Throttle/Brakes: Control speed.

Adaptive Cruise Control

Maintains a safe distance from the car in front.

1. Monitor
RADAR/Laser sensor measures distance to car ahead.
2. Calculate
Microprocessor calculates: Is Distance < Safe Distance?
3. Act
If too close: Actuator applies Brakes.
If clear: Actuator increases Throttle to set speed.

Case Study 3: Agriculture

How do you water a 10km² farm efficiently without wasting water?

Deep Dive: Smart Irrigation

Automated Irrigation

Inputs
Soil Moisture Sensors, Weather Station (Rain/Temp).
Process
Wireless transmitters send data to Controller. Controller checks: Is Soil Dry AND No Rain Forecast?
Outputs
Actuators open Water Valves and start Pumps.
Benefit: Saves water (precious resource) and reduces labour costs.

Case Study 4: Weather

Why do we use automated stations in the middle of the desert?

Deep Dive: Remote Sensing

Automated Weather Stations

The Setup

  • Thermometer: Temperature
  • Anemometer: Wind Speed
  • Hygrometer: Humidity
  • Barometer: Pressure

The Automation

Data is collected 24/7 and stored in a database. It is transmitted automatically via wireless/satellite.

No Actuators? Mostly no (except maybe a rain gauge tipper). It's a monitoring system.

Quick Fire Examples

Gaming

Accelerometer: Measures tilt/movement.

Action: Moves character on screen.

Lighting

Light Sensor: Detects darkness.

Action: Actuator switches on streetlights.

The Debate: To Automate or Not?

Advantages

  • Faster: Reacts in milliseconds.
  • Safer: Removes humans from danger (Nuclear).
  • Consistent: Does not get tired or make mistakes.
  • Cheaper (Long Term): Fewer wages to pay.

Disadvantages

  • Expensive (Setup): High initial cost for tech.
  • Maintenance: Complex repairs needed.
  • Security: Vulnerable to hacking.
  • Jobs: Replaces low-skilled labour.

True or False?

Answer these misconception checks in your book:

  1. "Automated systems eliminate the need for ALL human involvement."
  2. "An ADC is needed for a digital temperature sensor."
  3. "Automated irrigation saves water compared to manual watering."

Press Down to reveal answers

Question 1

"Automated systems eliminate the need for ALL human involvement."

Answer: False

Humans are still needed for monitoring, maintenance, and safety overrides.

Question 2

"An ADC is needed for a digital temperature sensor."

Answer: False

If the sensor is already digital, no conversion is needed. ADC is for analogue sensors.

Question 3

"Automated irrigation saves water compared to manual watering."

Answer: True

It only waters when necessary (soil is dry) and uses precise amounts.
