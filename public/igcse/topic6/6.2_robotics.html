Question 1

What are the 3 main components of an automated system?

Answer:

Sensors (Input), Microprocessor (Process), Actuators (Output).

Question 2

What is the purpose of the 'Feedback' loop?

Answer:

To compare the actual output (e.g., current temp) with the desired output (preset value) and adjust accordingly.

Question 3

Name one advantage of using automated systems in nuclear power.

Answer:

Safety (removes humans from radiation danger) or Speed (reacts faster than humans to emergencies).

The Robot Revolution

Sci-Fi

Terminators, C-3PO, and humanoid servants that look and act like us.

Reality

Mechanical arms welding cars, Roombas vacuuming floors, and drones delivering packages.

Key Idea: A robot is defined by what it does, not what it looks like.

What is a Robot?

Robot

A machine capable of carrying out a complex series of actions automatically, usually programmable by a computer.

To be a "true" robot, it typically needs three characteristics:


Sense
Sensors to perceive surroundings.

Move
Actuators/Mechanical parts to interact with the world.

Programmable
A controller ('brain') that follows instructions.
Deep Dive: Independent vs Dependent

Types of Robots

Independent (Autonomous)

No direct human control. Replaces human activity totally.

Example: Autonomous Vacuum Cleaner, Mars Rover.

Dependent

Human interfaces directly with the robot. Supplements human activity.

Example: Robotic Surgery Arm (controlled by surgeon), Car Assembly Cobot.

Note: Do not confuse physical robots with "Software Bots" (e.g., Search Engine Crawlers or Chatbots). They are not true robots in this context.

Case Study 1: Industry

Why have robots taken over car factories?

Tasks
  • Welding parts together.
  • Spray-painting panels.
  • Fitting windscreens (high precision).
  • Heavy lifting in warehouses.
How?

End Effectors: Different tools attached to the robot arm (e.g., Grippers, Spray Guns, Welders).

Deep Dive: Adv vs Disadv

Industry: Evaluation

Advantages

  • Consistency: Identical product every time.
  • Productivity: Work 24/7 without breaks.
  • Safety: Work in hazardous environments (fumes, heat).
  • Cost: Cheaper in the long run (fewer wages).

Disadvantages

  • Unemployment: Replaces manual labour jobs.
  • Deskilling: Humans lose the skills to do the work.
  • Setup Cost: Very expensive to buy and install.
  • Inflexibility: Difficult to reprogram for "non-standard" tasks.

Case Study 2: Transport

Would you trust a plane with no pilot?

Autonomous Cars
Use Lidar (Light Detection and Ranging), Radar, and Cameras to build a 3D map of the road.
Actuators control steering, braking, and throttle.
Autonomous Trains
Already common (e.g., Airport shuttles, London DLR).
Easier than cars because they follow a fixed track and don't overtake.
Unpiloted Planes
Most flying is already autopilot. Fully autonomous planes could remove the cockpit entirely for better aerodynamics.
Sensors detect turbulence and adjust flaps instantly.
Deep Dive: The Ethics & Issues

Transport: The Issues

Advantages

  • Safer: Removes human error (tiredness, distraction).
  • Efficient: Smoother driving saves fuel and reduces traffic jams.
  • Convenience: Stress-free parking; productive commute time.

Disadvantages

  • Hacking: Fear of terrorists taking control of vehicles.
  • Software Glitches: Bugs could be catastrophic.
  • Public Reluctance: Humans don't trust the technology yet.
  • Liability: Who is responsible in a crash? The owner or the coder?

Case Study 3: Agriculture

Farming 9 billion people requires robots, not just tractors.

Harvesting
Cameras identify ripe fruit. Soft grippers pick it without damage. Faster than humans.
Weed Control
AI distinguishes weeds from crops. Lasers or mechanical blades remove them. Reduces chemical use.
Drones
Aerial survey of crop health (Phenotyping). Precise spraying of fertiliser.

Question 1

"A drone controlled by a remote is an autonomous robot."

Answer: False

If it is remote-controlled by a human, it is a dependent robot. Autonomous means it flies itself.

Question 2

"Robots in industry generally lead to higher product consistency."

Answer: True

Robots perform the exact same movement every time, whereas humans might get tired or vary slightly.

Question 3

"Search engine 'bots' are a type of physical robot."

Answer: False

These are software programs. A robot generally implies a physical machine with sensors and actuators.
